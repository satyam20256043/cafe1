# Zordic — Pitch Playbook

The strategic approach behind every pitch — phone, in-person, or video. For the
**exact words to say on a phone call**, see `docs/ZORDIC_SALES_CALL_SCRIPT.md`;
this doc is the philosophy and reusable reference that script is built on.

**The core mistake to avoid:** explaining the product. Café owners care about
their business, not your features. Every step below exists to keep the
conversation about *them*, not about Zordic.

---

## The flow

### 1. Introduction
> "Hello sir/ma'am, I'm Satyam, founder of Zordic. We've built an AI platform
> specifically for cafés that helps increase repeat customers, automate
> customer communication, and grow revenue. I just wanted 5 minutes to show
> you something that might genuinely help your business."

### 2. Ask questions — get them talking
- How do customers usually contact you? WhatsApp or calls?
- Do you collect customer details after every visit?
- How do you bring customers back after their first visit?
- Do you run any loyalty or reward program?
- How do you promote offers?
- Do you know how many customers haven't visited in the last month?

Their answers become your whole pitch — you're about to repeat their own
words back to them.

### 3. Create the problem
> "Many cafés spend a lot on getting new customers, but most don't have a
> system to bring those customers back. Losing existing customers is often
> more expensive than acquiring new ones."

### 4. Introduce Zordic
> "That's exactly why we built Zordic. It's an AI Growth Platform that helps
> cafés get more repeat customers, automate WhatsApp conversations, manage
> customer relationships, run marketing campaigns, and understand their
> business through one dashboard."

Don't list every feature.

### 5. Talk benefits, not features
See the reframing table below — this is the single most important habit in
the whole pitch.

### 6. Show the demo
> "Let me quickly show you our presentation."

Share **zordic.in/pitch** and walk through it together.

### 7. Show the future
> "We're continuously improving the platform. Early partner cafés also get to
> influence which features we build next."

Makes them feel like a partner, not a customer number 4,000.

### 8. Close
> "Would you be interested in trying it in your café? We'd love to work
> closely with you and understand your requirements."

## Feature → benefit reframing

Never say the left column out loud. Always say the right column.

| Never say | Always say |
|---|---|
| "Our platform has AI." / "AI Chatbot" | "Customers receive instant replies even when your staff is busy — even outside business hours." |
| "We have CRM." / "Customer Database" | "You'll know which customers visit regularly and which ones haven't returned — instead of losing that information after every visit." |
| "Marketing Automation" | "You can automatically send offers to customers who haven't visited recently — the right offer to the right customer, not the same message to everyone." |
| "We have Analytics." / "Executive Dashboard" | "You'll know exactly what's increasing your revenue and what needs attention — no spreadsheets to maintain." |

## Talking points (use to back up the reframes above)

- **Customer retention** — existing customers are your biggest asset.
- **More repeat visits** — a customer who comes back every week is worth more than constantly finding new ones.
- **AI WhatsApp receptionist** — instant responses, even outside business hours.
- **Customer database** — finally know who your customers are.
- **Loyalty** — give customers a reason to come back.
- **Marketing** — target the right customers with the right offers, not a blast to everyone.
- **Executive dashboard** — business performance in one place, no spreadsheets.
- **Time saving** — staff focus on serving customers; routine communication runs itself.

## The one-line pitch

If they ask "So what exactly do you do?":

> "Zordic helps cafés get more repeat customers, automate customer
> communication, and grow revenue through AI — all from one platform."
