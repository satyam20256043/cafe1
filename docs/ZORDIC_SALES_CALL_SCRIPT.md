# Zordic — Phone Call Script & Conversation Guide

For calling café/restaurant owners. Read once fully, then keep the **Cheat Card** (bottom)
open during calls. Lines in quotes are meant to be said almost verbatim — but say them like
yourself, not like reading. Mix Hinglish naturally if the owner does.

For the strategic *why* behind this script — especially the feature→benefit reframing habit —
see `docs/ZORDIC_PITCH_PLAYBOOK.md`. Same rule applies everywhere below: never say "AI chatbot"
or "CRM," always say what it does for their business.

**Call goal, in order of preference:**
1. Sign them up live on the call (walk them through zordic.in/onboard together)
2. Book an in-person visit / demo at their café
3. Permission to send the pitch link + guide on WhatsApp and a fixed follow-up day

Never end a call without one of these three.

---

## 0. Before you dial (60 seconds)

- Look up the café on Google Maps: rating, review count, photos, busy times.
  One specific detail = ten generic pitches.
- Have open on your screen: `zordic.in/pitch`, `zordic.in/onboard`, this script.
- Ready to send on WhatsApp: pitch link, `zordic.in/Zordic-Owner-Guide.pdf`.
- Smile before you dial — it genuinely changes your voice.

---

## 1. The opener (first 20 seconds decide everything)

**Cold call:**
> "Hi, am I speaking with the owner of [Café Name]? ... Great — my name is Satyam,
> I run a small tech company called Zordic. We help cafés get more repeat customers
> using WhatsApp. I'm not selling a POS machine, I promise. Do you have 3 minutes,
> or should I call at a better time?"

Why it works: names their café (not a random list call), says the benefit in one line
(repeat customers — every owner wants this), the POS joke disarms, and asking permission
respects their time. If they say "busy" → **"No problem — is 4 PM okay, or tomorrow morning?"**
(offer two options, never "when are you free?").

**If a lead came from the website/referral:**
> "Hi [Name], you'd checked out Zordic online / [X] gave me your number — calling to
> personally answer any questions. What made you curious about it?"
Then go straight to Discovery — they'll tell you their pain themselves.

---

## 2. Discovery — 3 questions, then LISTEN (minutes 1–3)

Ask, then stay silent. Whatever pain they name becomes your whole pitch.

1. > "Quick question — when a customer WhatsApps you at 11 at night asking about a
   > table, what happens to that message?"
2. > "Are your weekdays as busy as your weekends, or is there a slow day that bugs you?"
3. > "When a regular customer stops coming — do you even get to know, or do they just
   > quietly disappear?"

Note their answer word-for-word. You will repeat their own words back in the pitch —
this is the single most persuasive thing you can do on a phone call.

---

## 3. The pitch (minutes 3–6) — lead with THEIR pain

**Bridge line (always):**
> "So what Zordic is — think of it as an AI receptionist that lives inside your
> WhatsApp number. Your customers message the same number they already have.
> The difference is: now every message gets answered in seconds, 24 hours a day."

Then pick the ONE lane matching their discovery answer:

**Lane A — they miss messages / bookings:**
> "That 11 PM message you mentioned? The AI answers it instantly — menu, prices,
> timings — and if they want a table, it books it, asks their name, guests, time,
> and it's on your dashboard before you wake up. You lose zero bookings to slow replies."

**Lane B — slow weekdays:**
> "You said Tuesdays are dead. Every Monday morning Zordic looks at your actual sales
> and sends you one suggestion — like 'Tuesday 3–6 PM, 15% off, send to your regulars.'
> You tap approve once, it goes to your customers on WhatsApp. That's your marketing
> department, for the price of a few coffees."

**Lane C — customers disappear:**
> "Zordic quietly notices every regular who hasn't come in 2 weeks, writes them a
> personal WhatsApp message with a small offer and a coupon code, and — this is the
> part owners love — you SEE who actually came back and spent money. Not likes,
> not views. Rupees."

**The safety line (use in every call — it kills the biggest fear before they say it):**
> "And important — the AI never gambles with your reputation. A complaint, a refund,
> a big group booking, anything it's not sure about? It calms the customer politely
> and immediately alerts YOU on WhatsApp with a suggestion. Big decisions stay yours.
> Even discounts — you set the maximum it's allowed to give, per item if you want."

**If they ask "what else does it do?" — the 30-second full tour:**
> "Full picture: AI receptionist on WhatsApp · QR codes on tables so guests order
> without waiting for staff · orders go straight to a kitchen screen · automatic
> loyalty points so visitors become regulars · win-back messages for lapsed customers ·
> a weekly growth suggestion from your own sales data · reviews — happy customers get
> nudged to Google, unhappy ones come privately to you first · and a dashboard where
> you see every order, every rupee, every conversation, live, from your phone."

**Numbers (say slowly, they're the hook):**
> "For a café doing about 60 customers a day, even our conservative math says
> ₹40–50,000 extra a month from repeat visits and saved bookings. The plan most
> cafés take is ₹3,000 a month. It pays for itself if it saves ONE table a week."

## 4. Objections — phone versions

**"I'm doing fine without it."**
> "That's honestly great to hear. Can I ask just one thing — [repeat their discovery
> answer, e.g. 'you did say night messages go unanswered']. If fixing only that was
> free for 30 days, worth a look?"

**"AI will say something wrong to my customers."**
> "Right instinct — here's the design: anything sensitive, it doesn't answer.
> It says 'let me check with the owner', and pings you. And you can watch every
> single conversation it has, live, in your dashboard. Nothing is hidden from you."

**"Too costly."**
> "What does an average table give you — ₹400? ₹500? So ₹3,000 is six tables a month.
> The win-back messages alone usually bring more than six people back. And the first
> 30 days are free with no card, so the risk is literally zero."

**"I'm not technical."**
> "Perfect, it's built for you then. If you can use WhatsApp, you can use Zordic.
> There's a full picture-guide for every button, and I personally help you set up —
> including the WhatsApp connection, which is the only technical step, and I do
> that WITH you."

**"Send me details, I'll see."** (the polite brush-off)
> "Of course, sending right now on this number. But so I send the right thing —
> what's the one thing you'd want it to prove before you'd trust it?"
Their answer = the real objection. Handle THAT, then close again.

**"Let me ask my partner/wife/brother."**
> "Makes sense. What do you think THEY'll ask? ... Let me give you the answer to
> that right now so you're ready." Then: "Should I call back tomorrow evening after
> you two talk — 7 PM?"

---

## 5. Closing (minutes 6–8) — momentum, not pressure

**Best close — sign up together, on the call:**
> "Tell you what — the signup takes literally 2 minutes and the first 30 days are
> free, no card. Open zordic.in/onboard on your phone, I'll stay on the line and
> we'll do it together right now. Then you can play with it tonight."

Walking them through it live is worth 10 follow-up calls. While on it:
name → owner name → phone → done → **"See that username and password? Screenshot it
right now."** → show them the dashboard opens.

**Second close — book the visit:**
> "Actually, better idea — I'm in [area] on [day]. Give me 15 minutes at your café,
> I'll set up your real menu in front of you and you can see a live order on your
> own table. [Day] morning or evening?"

**Soft close — locked follow-up:**
> "No problem. I'm sending you two links on WhatsApp — a 2-minute visual demo and
> the full guide. Have a look tonight, and I'll call Thursday at 5. If it's not for
> you, tell me straight, no hard feelings."

## 6. After the call — WhatsApp within 5 minutes (while you're warm in their memory)

**If signed up:**
> "Great talking, [Name]! 🎉 Your café is live. Your login: zordic.in/login/[cafe-id]
> Guide with pictures for every button: zordic.in/Zordic-Owner-Guide.pdf
> I'll call tomorrow to help connect your WhatsApp — that's the last step. Welcome aboard! ☕"

**If thinking:**
> "Good talking, [Name]! As promised:
> ▸ 2-min demo: zordic.in/pitch
> ▸ Full guide: zordic.in/Zordic-Owner-Guide.pdf
> The trial is 30 days free, no card. Calling you [day] at [time] as agreed. — Satyam, Zordic"

**If no:**
> "Thanks for your time today, [Name]. No follow-ups from me — but if slow Tuesdays
> ever start bugging you, this number works. All the best with the café! ☕"
(A graceful no today is a warm lead in 3 months. Owners talk to each other.)

**Didn't pick up — voicemail/missed-call text:**
> "Hi [Name], Satyam from Zordic here — we help cafés like [Café] turn WhatsApp
> messages into bookings automatically. Sending a 2-min demo link here. Will try
> you once tomorrow — or just reply with a good time. ☕"

## 7. Phone manner — the 8 rules

1. **Stand up or sit straight** — your voice carries energy or the lack of it.
2. **Slow down.** Nervous = fast. Trustworthy = unhurried.
3. **Pause after questions.** Count 3 in your head. Silence does the selling.
4. **Their name, 2–3 times** across the call. Not more (creepy), not zero (cold).
5. **Never talk over them.** The moment they speak, you stop, even mid-word.
6. **Match their language.** They go Hinglish, you go Hinglish.
7. **One lane per call.** Their pain, your fix. The other features are for later.
8. **Log every call** — name, pain, objection, next step, date promised. A promised
   callback made ON TIME converts better than any script line in this file.

## 8. Never do these (they backfire, simple as that)

- Claim a feature that doesn't exist or a client that doesn't exist.
- Invent urgency ("only 2 spots left"). The free trial and the math are enough.
- Argue with a hard no. Thank, exit warm, log it.
- Quote a discount you haven't decided you can honor.
- Bad-mouth Zomato/Swiggy/competitors — say instead: "we don't replace them,
  we make sure YOUR OWN customers come back to YOU, without commission."

## ☎️ CHEAT CARD (keep visible during calls)

**OPEN:** Owner? → Satyam, Zordic → more repeat customers via WhatsApp → "3 minutes?"
**ASK:** night messages? · slow day? · disappearing regulars? → LISTEN
**PITCH:** their pain → one lane (A bookings / B slow days / C win-back) → safety line → ₹ math
**SAFETY:** complaints/big groups/refunds → AI alerts YOU · you cap all discounts
**MATH:** ₹3,000/mo ≈ 6 tables · conservative +₹40–50k/mo · 30 days free, no card
**CLOSE:** onboard together NOW → or visit [day]? → or links + locked callback
**AFTER:** WhatsApp within 5 min · log the call · call back exactly when promised
